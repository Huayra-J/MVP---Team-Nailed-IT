Formal Writing

When writing an essay, it is important to construct a reasoned argument that is supported by carefully researched evidence.The language that you use needs to be precise and uncluttered by unnecessary devices.

  • Cliches
  • Metaphors

What to avoid?

Cliché

A cliché is an expression that has been overused. For example:

  • We cannot build a new school at this point in time.
  • Who would have thought that slavery could exist in this day and age?
  • The Prime Minister believed that at the end of the day her policies would be vindicated.

What to avoid?

Metaphors

A metaphor takes a name or descriptive term and applies it to a person or object in a non-literal sense.For example:

  • a glaring error
  • the heart of the matter
  • wave of terrorism
